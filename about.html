My story

Lucas King-Cochran is a student and service worker who experiences Ann Arbor's cost-of-living crisis firsthand — not in policy briefings, but in rent checks, grocery bills, and the daily reality of making ends meet in one of Michigan's most expensive cities.

He is running for Ann Arbor City Council in Ward 1 as an independent democratic socialist because he believes that working-class representation matters. For too long, City Hall has listened to corporate donors, real estate developers, and well-funded incumbents while students, service workers, renters, and longtime residents go unheard.

Corporate interests and privatization of city services have led to higher rent, higher taxes, and a lower quality of life. The streets of our historic neighborhoods are crumbling while we are assured that luxury apartments and hotels are the answer to our housing problem.

As your City Council member, Lucas pledges to fight for public housing, tenant and labor rights, and to protect our city's future from corporate greed, federal overreach, and climate change.

About the campaign

This campaign is a grassroots movement focused on making the voices of disenfranchised communities in Ward 1 heard. Lucas is running against an establishment, pro-police incumbent who raised more than $40,000 in her last campaign — and whose website still does not have a single tangible policy proposal.

No corporate money. No PAC backing. Just people power — and a commitment to the Ward 1 community.
